About Our Mission & The Challenge

Understanding the pervasive impact of unemployment and our commitment to sustainable community empowerment.

The Challenge of Unemployment in Our Community

Unemployment is a multi-faceted issue affecting individuals, families, and the broader economic and social fabric of our region.

In our community, unemployment rates present a significant hurdle to progress and well-being. Beyond the economic figures, it translates into real struggles for many, impacting mental health, increasing social inequalities, and hindering local development. The Employment Action Collective was founded on the belief that a collective, informed approach is essential to tackling this complex issue head-on.

"Unemployment is not just an economic indicator; it's a social determinant of health, prosperity, and community stability. Ignoring it means undermining our collective future."
- Local Community Leader

Key Facts & Figures

Data-driven insights highlight the urgency and specific areas of concern in our community.

15% Unemployment Rate

Our community's current unemployment rate significantly surpasses the national average, indicating a concentrated challenge.

Youth Disproportionately Affected

Over 40% of unemployed individuals are under 30, highlighting a critical need for youth-focused interventions.

Increased Mental Health Issues

Studies show a direct correlation between prolonged unemployment and a 3x increase in reported anxiety and depression.

Skills Gap in Key Industries

A significant mismatch exists between the skills job seekers possess and those required by growing local sectors.
